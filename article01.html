Amsterdam In Bloom

5 reasons why it's the most liveable city

photography & story
JAYSON ONG
1.Good Food.

The Dutch fight food wastage by picking up unsold products at local supermarkets and producers, as ingredients in this restuarant - think of products like blemished fruits and vegetables, one-day-old bread and meat and fish surplus. It keeps the kitchen creative, constantly improvising and coming up with a different menu everyday based on the “harvest of the day”. Giving good food a deeper meaning.

instock.nl | Czaar Peterstraat 21, Amsterdam | +31 (0)20 363 5765

2.Dutch Art.

The Rijksmuseum is a Dutch national museum has on display 8,000 objects of art and history, from their total collection of 1 million objects from the years 1200–2000, among which are some masterpieces by Rembrandt, Frans Hals, and Johannes Vermeer. The must-see being The Night Watch (1642) by Rembrandt - for its splendour can only be appreciated in person.

rijksmuseum.nl | Museumstraat 1, 1071 XX Amsterdam | +31 20 674 7000

3.Flowers.

One of the world’s largest flower gardens, Keukenhof is situated in Lisse. Approximately 7 million flower bulbs are planted annually in the park, which covers an area of 32 hectares (79 acres). Keukenhof is open annually from mid-March to mid-May. The best time to view the tulips is around mid-April, depending on the weather.

keukenhof.nl | Stationsweg 166A, Lisse | +31 252 465 555

4.Instagram Worthy.

Endearingly known as the “Venice of the North”, the city has more than one hundred kilometers of canals, about 90 islands and 1,500 bridges. Making it an extremely picturesque city at every turn. Then cityscape changes constantly with the seasons/time of day, so shutterbugs be warn - pack those extra memory cards just in case.

5.Cycle Everywhere.

Cycling is the preferred mode of transport here. The city is well-connected and a few hours on the bike allows one to explore quite a fair bit of the town center. From snacks bars to cafes, there’s a huge array of food in this cosmopolitan city to satisfy the pickiest eaters. Will ride for food, please.
